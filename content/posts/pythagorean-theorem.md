# The Pythagorean Theorem

One of the most famous theorems in geometry is the **Pythagorean Theorem**, named after the ancient Greek mathematician **Pythagoras**.

It applies specifically to **right-angled triangles** (triangles with one $90^\circ$ angle).

---

## 📐 The Theorem Statement

In any right-angled triangle:

> *"The square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides."*

$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$

- $c$ = **Hypotenuse** (the longest side, opposite to the $90^\circ$ right angle)
- $a$ and $b$ = **Base** and **Perpendicular** (the two legs forming the right angle)

---

## 🧠 Common Pythagorean Triples

A **Pythagorean triple** consists of three positive integers $(a, b, c)$ that satisfy $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

| Triple $(a, b, c)$ | Verification |
| :--- | :--- |
| **(3, 4, 5)** | $3^2 + 4^2 = 9 + 16 = 25 = 5^2$ |
| **(5, 12, 13)** | $5^2 + 12^2 = 25 + 144 = 169 = 13^2$ |
| **(8, 15, 17)** | $8^2 + 15^2 = 64 + 225 = 289 = 17^2$ |
| **(7, 24, 25)** | $7^2 + 24^2 = 49 + 576 = 625 = 25^2$ |

---

## 📝 Example Problem

### Problem:
A right triangle has legs of length $a = 6\text{ cm}$ and $b = 8\text{ cm}$. Find the length of the hypotenuse $c$.

### Solution:
1. Apply the formula:
   $$c^2 = a^2 + b^2$$
2. Substitute $a = 6$ and $b = 8$:
   $$c^2 = 6^2 + 8^2$$
   $$c^2 = 36 + 64 = 100$$
3. Take the square root:
   $$c = \sqrt{100} = 10\text{ cm}$$

Answer: The hypotenuse is **$10\text{ cm}$**.

---

## 🏃 Real-World Application: Ladder Against a Wall

Suppose a 13-foot ladder leans against a building. If the bottom of the ladder is 5 feet away from the base of the building, how high up the building does the ladder reach?

1. Hypotenuse $c = 13\text{ ft}$, base $a = 5\text{ ft}$, height $h = ?$
2. $5^2 + h^2 = 13^2$
3. $25 + h^2 = 169$
4. $h^2 = 169 - 25 = 144$
5. $h = \sqrt{144} = 12\text{ ft}$

The ladder reaches **$12\text{ ft}$** high!
