# Fractions, Decimals & Percentages

Fractions, decimals, and percentages are three different ways of representing the **same value**!

---

## 🔄 Quick Conversion Table

| Fraction | Decimal | Percentage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| $\frac{1}{2}$ | $0.5$ | $50\%$ |
| $\frac{1}{4}$ | $0.25$ | $25\%$ |
| $\frac{3}{4}$ | $0.75$ | $75\%$ |
| $\frac{1}{5}$ | $0.2$ | $20\%$ |
| $\frac{1}{10}$ | $0.1$ | $10\%$ |

---

## 1. Converting Fractions to Decimals

To convert a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$ into a decimal, divide the numerator ($a$) by the denominator ($b$).

### Example:
Convert $\frac{3}{8}$ into a decimal:
$$3 \div 8 = 0.375$$

---

## 2. Converting Decimals to Percentages

To convert a decimal into a percentage, **multiply by 100** (shift the decimal point 2 places to the right).

### Examples:
- $0.45 \times 100 = 45\%$
- $0.08 \times 100 = 8\%$
- $1.25 \times 100 = 125\%$

---

## 3. Practice Problem: Discount Calculation

Imagine a math textbook costs <span>$</span>40. The bookstore offers a $15\%$ discount. How much money do you save?

### Solution:
1. Convert $15\%$ into a decimal: $15\% = \frac{15}{100} = 0.15$
2. Multiply by the original price:
   $$\text{Discount} = 0.15 \times 40 = \$6$$
3. Final Price = <span>$</span>40 - <span>$</span>6 = <span>$</span>34.

You save **<span>$</span>6**!
